        The idea
        
Hobbyist GPUs, on the air.

        
There's a whole community running powerful open models on hardware at home.
          RogerAI turns that idle capacity into a shared band - discoverable, fair, and
          dead-simple to join from either side.

      


      

        

          

            
            
            go on air
          

          
Share a model

          
Point RogerAI at your local backend (vLLM, llama.cpp, Ollama). Set a call-sign,
            a price, and the hours you're around. Flip the switch - you're on air.

          rogerai share waterfall --rate 0.30
        


        

          
        


        

          

            
            
            tune in
          

          
Use a model

          
Browse stations by signal, price, and distance. Tune in and RogerAI hands you a
            local OpenAI endpoint + key - a drop-in for anything that speaks OpenAI.

          rogerai connect @nightowl
        

      

    

  


  
  

    

      

        How it works
        
Flexible by design. Easy by default.

      


      

        

          

            
            
          

          
On air / tune in

          
Two verbs. Owners broadcast a model; users tune to a channel. Signal bars are
            tokens per second, so good and bad are obvious at a glance.

        


        

          

            
            
          

          
Lineage receipts

          
A gold call-sign ◆ means every token is signed with its
            lineage - base weights, fine-tunes, and the exact station that aired it.

        


        

          

            
            
          

          
Your price, your terms

          
Set per-token rates per model - or share for free. Open only during your hours,
            cap usage, throttle by time of day. The market clears the rest.

        


        

          

            
            
          

          
Only the model. Nothing else.

          
RogerAI bridges the LLM endpoint and only that. No shell, no files, no context,
            no personal data - ever reachable. The rest of your machine stays yours.
