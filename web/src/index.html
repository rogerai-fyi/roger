§1 / OPERATING PROCEDURE

Tune in. Use it. Go on air.

One CLI does both sides of the radio: borrow a model from someone's home GPU, or lend your own and get paid per token. Pick a tape below and watch it run.

        §3 / THE TUNE
        
Home GPUs, on the air.

        
An operator puts a model on the air at a price they set; you tune in and
          pay per token; every token is lineage-tracked. Fair, private, and
          dead-simple from either side.

      


      

        

          
go on air

          
Share a model

          
Point RogerAI at your local backend (vLLM, llama.cpp, Ollama, LM Studio). Set a call-sign,
            a rate, and your hours. Flip the switch. You are on air.

          roger share qwen3-coder-30b --rate 0.30
        


        

          
        


        

          
tune in

          
Use a model

          
Pick a band, set your margins (max price, min speed). RogerAI locks the strongest
            station and hands you a local OpenAI endpoint + key. When one fades, it re-tunes.

          roger use qwen3-coder-30b --max-price 0.30 --min-tps 40

        §5 / OPERATING NOTES
        
Four notes before you transmit.

      


      

        

          01
          
Lock and hold

          
Tune with margins. The radio locks the strongest station and re-tunes when one fades.

        

        

          02
          
Sign every token ◆

          
A call-sign co-signs each token's lineage. ed25519, verifiable.

        

        

          03
          
Set your own rate

          
Per-token rates per model, free hours, time-of-use, usage caps. You decide.

        

        

          04
          
Only the model

          
RogerAI bridges the LLM endpoint, nothing else. No shell, files, or context. On the confidential ◆ tier, traffic routes only to nodes that pass real TEE hardware attestation (AMD SEV-SNP), so the operator cannot read the payload in memory.
