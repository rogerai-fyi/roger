8. Operator earnings, the platform fee, and payouts

The split. When an operator's node serves a paid request, the operator earns 70% of the metered cost of that request and RogerAI retains 30% as its platform fee. The 70/30 split is applied to RogerAI's authoritative metered figure (section 11). We may change the fee for future usage with notice; the fee in effect when a request is served governs that request's earning.

Earnings lifecycle. An earning moves through accrued → held → payable → paid. Each earning is held for 120 days from the request's settlement before it becomes payable. Why the hold exists: a consumer card charge can be disputed or reversed for up to roughly 120 days after it is made, and the platform is liable for that reversal (section 9). Holding earnings for 120 days means a reversed charge can almost always be clawed back from a still-held earning before the money has left Stripe, rather than chased from an operator after payout. We do not withhold a separate rolling reserve; the hold is the reserve.

Payout terms. Payouts run through Stripe Connect and require the operator to complete Stripe Connect onboarding and identity verification (KYC) - the transfers capability on the connected account must be active before any money moves out. A payable balance must reach a $25 minimum before a payout can be requested, and payable balances are released on a monthly batch. Payouts go only to the operator's own KYC-verified connected account.

Who moves the money. RogerAI uses Stripe Connect with separate charges and transfers: consumers are charged by Stripe, and operator earnings are sent to operators as Stripe Transfers to their connected accounts. Stripe is the regulated money services business and the entity that holds and transmits funds. RogerAI is a technology platform and marketplace that instructs Stripe; it is not a bank and is not itself a money transmitter, and it does not hold customer funds.
