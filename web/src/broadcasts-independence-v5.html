Independence Release Broadcast · v5.0.0

Own your GPU. Own your models. Hold your own signed receipts. The v5.0.0 release is about independence from metered Big-AI clouds - your station, your band, your terms.

Release: v5.0.0 On air: July 4 Set: rogerai Broker: broker.rogerai.fyi

v5.0.0 is the Independence Release, and it goes on the air July 4. The whole point of RogerAI has always been that the compute already exists - on your desk, in your closet, on a friend's rig - and it should answer to you, not to a meter you do not control. This release makes that concrete.

§0 · On the dial in v5.0.0

What's new.

  • Base Station remote control. Put your live agent session on the air with /remote-control, then pick it up from any terminal, the web Base Station console, or a one-time link on your phone. One session, one transcript, every surface sees the same conversation, and tools still run only on your host, only after a y/N confirm you can answer from any attached device.
  • Price tiers at a glance. Every model on the dial now carries a neutral $ to $$$$ read, or FREE, so you can gauge a fair price for the same model in one look. Operators still set their own prices; the dial just tells you how they compare.
  • First-class voices. Browse voice stations on the air, cue a shared DJ in TUNE IN, speak through any voice with roger say, and share your own from the VOICE BOOTH, namespaced and attributed to your station.
  • One dollar to start. The $1 starter credit now greets you from the first screen, so you can tune in before you ever add a card.
  • Hardening across the band. Faster first responses by skipping unproven nodes, refund clawback that settles the ledger honestly, voice and transcription guardrails, and a CyberTipline compliance surface. Quiet work, but independence runs on trust.
§1 · Own your silicon

Your GPU, your models.

The card in your machine is already paid for. RogerAI turns it into a station: run the open weights you choose - Llama, Qwen, gpt-oss, your own fine-tune - and answer on your terms. Nobody rents you back access to intelligence your own hardware can already run. The models are open, the silicon is yours; all RogerAI adds is the dial between them.

§2 · No lock-in

No lock-in.

RogerAI speaks plain, OpenAI-compatible HTTP through one small open binary. Point your existing code at the broker and it just works - and the day you'd rather self-host, go direct, or walk away entirely, nothing holds you: no proprietary SDK, no data moat, no egress toll. An open band is a door that swings both ways, and we built it to open outward.

§3 · Signed receipts you hold

Receipts you hold.

Every token is metered and lineage-tracked, and every request settles as an Ed25519 receipt - signed by the node that served it, counter-signed by the broker that verified it, kept in your wallet. You don't take our word for the bill; you hold the proof. The ledger is append-only and yours to audit. Trust, but verify - here you actually can.

§4 · Run your own station

Run your own station.

Going on air is one command - roger share - and your box becomes a station on the dial, discoverable by call-sign, keeping 70% of every token it serves (or free, if you'd rather just feed the commons). Set your price, your hours, your models; mint private grant keys for your own bots or your family. It's your frequency - broadcast what you want on it.

§5 · Cut the cord

Independent from the metered clouds.

Big AI's deal is fixed: their silicon, their meter always running, your data passing through, models deprecated out from under you on their schedule. Independence is the inverse - a peer-to-peer band of home GPUs, run by the people who own them and answerable to the people who use them, with no single company standing between you and the models. On the Fourth of July, that felt worth naming. Find your frequency, and tune in.
