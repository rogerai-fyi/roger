Privacy

What we collect, why, and how to get it out or wipe it. Written plainly.

Who we are

RogerAI is the platform behind rogerai.fyi and the broker at broker.rogerai.fyi. It routes prompts to self-hosted nodes and settles pay-per-token between consumers and node operators. This page covers the data the platform itself handles. Stripe (our payments provider) and GitHub (our login provider) handle data under their own policies, linked below. Groq (our moderation provider) also processes prompt text for a transient safety screen, described under "Prompt content and the safety screen".

Account and identity

You sign in with GitHub OAuth. From GitHub we receive and store your numeric GitHub user id and your login (handle), and - only if you choose to save one - a contact email. We do not receive or store your GitHub password. Your avatar is loaded directly from GitHub's public avatar CDN by your numeric id; we do not copy or host it.

Payments and payouts

Stripe is the payments provider and the regulated money services business that moves the funds. Top-ups run through Stripe Checkout and operator payouts through Stripe Connect after a 120-day hold and Stripe KYC. We never see, store, or process your card number, bank details, or KYC identity documents - Stripe holds all of that. On our side we keep only a wallet balance, a ledger of top-ups and spend, accrued earnings (your 70% share after the 30% platform fee), and the Stripe account/reference ids needed to reconcile a payment. For operators, tax and reporting obligations (for example a US Form 1099) may require Stripe and/or us to collect tax information and report earnings to the relevant tax authority; the money, fee, and tax terms are in the Terms.

Usage and lineage

When you send a request we record what is needed to bill it and to settle the operator: the model or node used, token counts, cost, and a timestamp. Settled requests produce a lineage receipt - a signed record of which node served which request - so both sides can audit the ledger. We do not sell your data or use prompt contents to train models.

Prompt content and the safety screen

The broker is content-blind for the marketplace. For a settled request it persists only token counts, cost, and hashes in the signed receipt - it does not store or read your prompt or the response text, and it never uses them to train models. The one honest exception is a transient, pre-dispatch safety screen: before a prompt is relayed to a node, its text is checked against a moderation model so we can refuse content we do not allow on the network (see the Terms and the Acceptable Use Policy). That screen runs in transit - the prompt is not stored for it - but it does mean the text is read by the screen at dispatch time, and is sent to Groq (below), our moderation provider, as a third party for that check.

The one mandatory exception: child sexual abuse material (CSAM). If the safety screen flags a prompt as apparent CSAM, US law (18 USC 2258A) requires us to preserve the offending request and report it to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC). In that single case the flagged request is preserved encrypted and access-controlled for a limited period, under a pseudonym, solely to meet that legal obligation and to distinguish repeat offenders. This is the only situation in which we retain prompt content, and we have zero tolerance for CSAM (see the Acceptable Use Policy in the Terms).

Ping, the on-site concierge. The little chat assistant on the website answers questions about RogerAI. It first tries a free on-air community station; if none is available it falls back to Groq. So a message you type into Ping may be sent to a community node or to Groq to produce a reply. Do not put secrets into Ping - it is a public, unauthenticated demo surface, not a private channel.

Cookies

We use a single cookie: a signed, http-only session cookie set by the broker after you log in. It is not readable by JavaScript and no access tokens are ever exposed to the page. We do not use advertising or third-party tracking cookies. Your browser may send GitHub's own cookies when loading your avatar from GitHub's CDN; that is GitHub's cookie, not ours.

What we log

Operationally we keep request and billing records (above), plus standard server logs needed to run and secure the service - for example error traces and abuse/fraud signals. We retain de-identified financial records for the legal retention window (tax and anti-money-laundering rules - typically up to 7 years) even after an account is deleted.

Your data: export and delete

You control your data from the Account page.

Export. "Export my data" calls POST /account/export and downloads a JSON copy of the account data we hold for you.

Delete. "Delete account" calls POST /account/delete. It anonymizes your identity (your GitHub id, login, and email are removed or scrambled) while retaining de-identified financial records for the legal window described above. The action cannot be undone.

Third parties

The processors we rely on: GitHub (login and avatars), Stripe (payments, payouts, KYC), Groq (the pre-dispatch safety screen described above, and Ping's fallback - prompt text is sent to Groq for those checks and replies), and our hosting provider (serving the site and broker). Each handles your data under its own privacy policy.

Contact

Questions, an export problem, or a data request: email privacy@rogerai.fyi. For security issues see the Security page. To report abuse or a station, email abuse@rogerai.fyi or use the report control on the Models directory.
